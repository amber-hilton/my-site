Research

Black Information Futures & Emerging Technologies

Research that centers Black communities, their information practices, and the possibilities and risks of AI and machine learning in public library contexts.

Research Themes

  • Affective and emotional dimensions of information seeking in marginalized communities
  • Black information futures and speculative approaches to librarianship
  • Public libraries as sites of technological literacy, resistance, and imagination
  • AI and machine learning as infrastructure shaping access to information

Approach

Amber’s research combines critical information studies, community-engaged methods, and practice-based insights from public librarianship. She is interested in how people actually navigate systems, not just how those systems are designed on paper.

Her work asks: What futures become possible when Black experiences, care, and imagination are centered in the design of library services and technologies?
