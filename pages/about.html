About

Amber Hilton

MLIS graduate student, public library branch manager, and researcher working at the intersection of community-centered librarianship, Black information futures, and emerging technologies.

Professional Background

Amber is a public library branch manager whose work centers on building welcoming, inclusive spaces where community members can explore ideas, access resources, and imagine new futures for themselves and their neighborhoods.

As an MLIS graduate student, she studies the affective and social dimensions of information seeking, with particular attention to how Black communities navigate systems that were not designed with them in mind.

Research & Practice

Amber’s research interests include:

  • Black information futures and speculative approaches to librarianship
  • Information behavior in marginalized and under-resourced communities
  • AI and machine learning in public library contexts
  • Ethical and community-informed uses of emerging technologies

Her practice as a branch manager informs her research questions, and her research in turn shapes the programs and partnerships she develops.

Values

Amber’s work is grounded in:

  • Equity and justice in access to information
  • Care-centered, relationship-based librarianship
  • Transparency and critical engagement with technology
  • Joy, creativity, and community imagination

Beyond the Library

Outside of formal roles, Amber explores ideas through creative projects, including her podcast Messy but Trying, writing, and collaborative work with other library workers and scholars.

This site gathers her programming work, research projects, and ongoing experiments in one place for collaborators, colleagues, and community to explore.
